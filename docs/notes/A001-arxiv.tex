\begin{abstract}
We study the three-dimensional Keller map \(F\) announced by Alp\"oge (with Fable credited): \(\det DF\equiv -2\) and a three-point collision. Theorem~A restates those finite identities; novelty is Theorems~B--F. The cotangent lift \(\Phi(q,p)=(F(q),J^{-T}p)\) is a polynomial Poisson map and non-injective. Dual fields \(X_j=\sum_k B_{jk}\partial_{q_k}\) commute and are divergence-free (Piola), so \(H_j=-iX_j\) are symmetric on \(C_c^\infty(\mathbb{R}^3)\). The field \(X_1\) is incomplete. In flow-box coordinates \((a,s,c)=F(q)\) one has \(X_1=\partial_s\) and \(\mathrm{d}q=\tfrac12\,\mathrm{d}a\,\mathrm{d}s\,\mathrm{d}c\). Open forward and backward escape walls yield whole-orbit deficiency vectors in \(\operatorname{Dom}(H^*)\) (transverse cutoffs only; no interior \(s\)-indicators), hence
\[
(n_+,n_-)=(\infty,\infty)
\]
for \(H=-iX_1\). Infinitely many self-adjoint extensions exist; algebraic CCR/Poisson data select none. No gates, channels, computational advantage, or seed discovery claim.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
On 19--20 July 2026, Levent Alp\"oge announced \(F:\mathbb{C}^3\to\mathbb{C}^3\) with \(\det DF\equiv-2\) and a three-point collision (credits Fable)~\cite{Alpoge2026}. \textbf{We do not claim discovery of \(F\).} We ask whether algebraic Poisson/CCR lifts select a unique self-adjoint dual transport generator on \(C_c^\infty(\mathbb{R}^3)\). Answer: no for \(H=-iX_1\); deficiency indices \((\infty,\infty)\).

\section{Flow-box coordinates}
On local inverse sheets, \((a,s,c)=F(q)\) gives
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{d}q=\tfrac12\,\mathrm{d}a\,\mathrm{d}s\,\mathrm{d}c,\qquad X_1=\partial_s,\qquad H=-i\partial_s.
\tag{7.1}
\end{equation}
Elimination yields \(A(s;a,c)q_0^3+B(s;c)q_0+C(c)=0\) with
\(A=-cs^3+s^2+18acs-27a^2c^2-16a\), \(B=3cs-4\), \(C=2c\).

\paragraph{Reconstruction.}
If \(x=q_0\) solves the cubic and denominators are nonzero,
\[
q_1=\frac{9acx^2-3csx-3c-sx^2+6x}{x((3cs-4)x+3c)},\quad
q_2=\frac{2x-3x^2q_1-c}{x^3},
\]
then \(F(x,q_1,q_2)=(a,s,c)\).

\section{Theorems E--F}
\paragraph{Dictionary.}
On \((\ell,r)\): finite upper end \(\Rightarrow n_-=1\) (model \(e^{s-r}\)); finite lower end \(\Rightarrow n_+=1\) (model \(e^{-(s-\ell)}\)).

\begin{proposition}[Forward wall]
At \((0,\tfrac12,2)\), \(A=0\), \(\partial_sA=-\tfrac12\), \(B=-1\). IFT gives wall \(\beta\). Set \(\tau=\sqrt{\beta-s}\), \(q_0=r/\tau\), and
\[
G_+=\frac{A(\beta-\tau^2)}{\tau^2}r^3+B(\beta-\tau^2)r+C\tau,
\]
extended at \(\tau=0\) by \((-A_s)r^3+Br\). Simple roots \(r=\pm\sqrt2\) (\(\partial_rG_+=-2B\neq0\)) continue by IFT; reconstruct by the formulas above.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proposition}[Backward wall]
At \(a=\tfrac1{54}\), \(c=2\), wall \(\alpha\) with \(A_s=\tfrac16\), \(B=-1\). Set \(\tau=\sqrt{s-\alpha}\) and analogous \(G_-\) extended by \(A_sr^3+Br\). Simple roots \(r=\pm\sqrt6\) continue by IFT on an open transverse set.
\end{proposition}

\begin{lemma}[Saturated maximal sheets]
Cross-section the selected branch below/above the wall; saturate under the flow of \(X_1\). Since \(X_1F_1=1\), \(s\) is time. ODE theory yields a \(C^\infty\) diffeomorphism \(\Psi_+\) from
\[
D_+=\{(a,c,s):(a,c)\in W_+\Subset U_+,\;\ell_+(a,c)<s<\beta(a,c)\}
\]
onto an open invariant set \(\Omega_+\subset\mathbb{R}^3\), with \(F\circ\Psi_+=(a,s,c)\). The end \(s\to\beta^-\) is at spatial infinity. If \(\ell_+\) is finite, maximality forces escape as \(s\downarrow\ell_+\) as well. Analogous \(\Psi_-,\Omega_-\) for the lower wall.
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}[Deficiency vectors in \(\operatorname{Dom}(H^*)\)]
For \(\chi\in C_c^\infty(W_+)\) define on the \emph{entire} maximal interval
\[
u_-(\Psi_+(a,c,s))=\chi(a,c)\,e^{s-\beta(a,c)},
\]
and \(u_-=0\) off \(\Omega_+\). No interior \(s\)-cutoff. Then
\[
\|u_-\|_2^2=\tfrac12\int_{W_+}|\chi|^2\int_{\ell_+}^{\beta}e^{2(s-\beta)}\,ds\,da\,dc\le\tfrac14\|\chi\|_{L^2}^2.
\]
For \(\varphi\in C_c^\infty(\mathbb{R}^3)\), \(\operatorname{supp}\varphi\) meets orbits in compact \(s\)-segments strictly inside \((\ell_+,\beta)\). Integration by parts with \(\operatorname{div}X_1=0\) gives no jump and no endpoint terms, so \(u_-\in\operatorname{Dom}(H^*)\) and \((H^*+i)u_-=0\). Thus \(n_-=\infty\). Likewise
\[
u_+(\Psi_-(a,c,s))=\chi(a,c)\,e^{-(s-\alpha(a,c))}
\]
on \((\alpha,r_-)\) yields \(n_+=\infty\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{theorem}[F]
\((n_+,n_-)=(\infty,\infty)\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The two walls, saturation, and deficiency lemmas; separability.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[E]
\(H\) is not essentially self-adjoint on \(C_c^\infty(\mathbb{R}^3)\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
\(n_\pm\ge1\).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
Infinitely many self-adjoint extensions exist. Core algebraic Poisson/CCR relations do not distinguish among them.
\end{corollary}

\section{Discussion}
The example separates two layers that are often collapsed in practice. On the algebraic side, the Poisson cotangent lift and the dual-field/CCR identities are clean: brackets close, rows of \(B\) are divergence-free, and \(H_j=-iX_j\) are symmetric on \(C_c^\infty(\mathbb{R}^3)\). On the analytic side, \emph{all three} dual fields \(X_j\) are incomplete, so each \(H_j=-iX_j\) fails to be essentially self-adjoint. For the middle momentum one obtains
\[
(n_+,n_-)=(\infty,\infty)
\quad(H_1),
\]
while the one-sided wall/omission models give
\[
(n_+,n_-)=(\infty,0)\ \text{for }H_0
\quad\text{and}\quad
(0,\infty)\ \text{for }H_2
\]
(so \(H_0\) and \(H_2\) admit \emph{no} self-adjoint extensions of the minimal operators). A working algebraic CCR/Poisson presentation therefore does \emph{not} select unique quantum dual momenta.

Moreover, no reducing subspace of \(L^2(\mathbb{R}^3)\) can carry unitary groups implementing dual \(F\)-translations in all three directions with open \(F\)-support (H4-S pilot no-go): the same non-surjectivity of \(F\) blocks that repair. Dual-flow strong CCR on this Hilbert space is obstructed. Roadmap Phases 1--3 classify the dual-flow Schrödinger program as a \textbf{no-go}; remaining options are other realization classes (dilation, nonregular representations, open systems) under a separate H4 charter, not a fix of \(H_j=-iX_j\) on \(C_c^\infty\).

This is not a claim that essential self-adjointness fails for every incomplete vector field in full generality: incomplete flows and half-line models are classical~\cite{RS2}. The point is geometric and structural for this Keller dual lift.

What the paper does \emph{not} establish is equally important. We do not claim discovery of \(F\); unitary gates, channels, or computational advantage; a preferred boundary condition; or that every abstract quantization route fails. Lean certification covers the seed identities (Theorem~A) and the adjugate identity for \(DF\); full G2--G4 formalization remains open.

\section{Non-claims}
No unitary gate, channel, CP map, or computational advantage. No dual-flow strong CCR. No preferred physical extension. No claim all quantization routes fail. Discovery of \(F\) not claimed. Lean: seed + adjugate identity only.
