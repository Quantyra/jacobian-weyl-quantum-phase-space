\begin{abstract}
Let \(\psi\) be the polynomial Weyl endomorphism of the Alp\"oge--Fable seed
(restated): \(Q=F(q)\), \(P=J^{-T}p\). With the physics normalization
\([q_i,p_j]=i\delta_{ij}\) and \(q_i^*=q_i\), \(p_j^*=p_j\), \(\psi\) is a unital
\(*\)-endomorphism (algebraic SOS positivity). We construct its algebraic
endomorphism correspondence and obstruct: (i)~unital/\allowbreak nondegenerate
\(*\)-endomorphisms of \(C_0(\mathbb{R}^3)\) extending composition with non-proper
\(F\); (ii)~Bogoliubov/quasifree endomorphisms of the CCR algebra (nonlinear
\(F\)). A former ``Free-Strict-Regular-v2'' nondegeneracy obstruction is
\textbf{withdrawn} as incorrect. We then \textbf{construct} a genuine normal
unital \(*\)-homomorphism (Koopman pullback) on the bounded-Borel position
sector, valid for any local-diffeomorphism \(F\) regardless of properness or
surjectivity. Extending this to the full \(q\)--\(p\) endomorphism \(\psi\)
(joint Stinespring dilation) remains open. No dual \(F\)-translations, gates,
or advantage. Companion to~\cite{A001}. Pack: \texttt{COMPANION-PACK.md}.
\end{abstract}

\section{Domain}
\begin{definition}
\(\mathcal{W}\): polynomial Weyl algebra over \(\mathbb{C}\) with
\([q_i,p_j]=i\delta_{ij}\), \([q_i,q_j]=[p_i,p_j]=0\), and involution
\(q_i^*=q_i\), \(p_j^*=p_j\). (Consistent: \([q,p]^*=-[q,p]\) and
\((i\delta)^*=-i\delta\).) With real \(B=J^{-T}\),
\[
\psi(q_i)=F_i(q),\qquad
\psi(p_j)=\sum_k B_{jk}(q)\,p_k
\]
(coefficients left of momenta) extends to a unital algebra endomorphism.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Normalization]
Earlier drafts used \([q,p]=\delta\) with self-adjoint generators, which is
inconsistent under \(*\). This note uses the standard physics normalization
\([q,p]=i\).
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[Algebraic \(*\)-SOS]
Assume \(F,B\) real and the Piola/\allowbreak dual-field identity \(\operatorname{div} B=0\)
(row divergence free; true for the A001 cotangent lift). Then each
Schr\"odinger operator \(H_j=-iX_j\) is \emph{symmetric} on \(C_c^\infty(\mathbb{R}^3)\)
(not claimed essentially self-adjoint---cf.~\cite{A001} for \(H_1\)).
At the algebraic level the formal adjoint correction
\(-i\sum_k\partial_k B_{jk}\) vanishes, so
\[
P_j^* = P_j
\quad\text{in }\mathcal{W}
\]
with the physics involution. Hence \(\psi\) is a unital \(*\)-endomorphism and
\(\mathrm{id}_n\otimes\psi\) preserves hermitian-square cones.
\end{theorem}

\begin{definition}[Algebraic correspondence]
Let \(E=\mathcal{W}\) as a \emph{right} \(\mathcal{W}\)-module by \(\xi\cdot b=\xi b\),
with \emph{left} action \(a\cdot\xi=\psi(a)\xi\) and
\(\langle\xi,\eta\rangle_{\mathcal{W}}=\xi^*\eta\).
\end{definition}

\section{Obstructions (retained)}
\begin{theorem}[C0 composition endo]
There is no \(*\)-homomorphism \(\alpha:C_0(\mathbb{R}^3)\to C_0(\mathbb{R}^3)\)
(nondegenerate/\allowbreak approximately unital in the usual \(C_0\) sense) with
\(\alpha(f)=f\circ F\) for all \(f\in C_0(\mathbb{R}^3)\), because \(F\) is not proper
so \(f\circ F\notin C_0\) for some \(f\in C_0\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Bogoliubov / quasifree]
No Bogoliubov (linear quasifree) endomorphism of the CCR \(C^*\)-algebra---i.e.\ a
map induced by a linear symplectic contraction on the one-particle space,
acting on Weyl unitaries by \(W(z)\mapsto W(Tz)\)---can reproduce the Heisenberg
picture dynamics of \(\psi\) on the Weyl one-parameter groups associated with the
field coordinates in a regular representation: those Bogoliubov maps act
linearly on one-particle fields, while \(q\mapsto F(q)\) is a nonlinear
polynomial map (total degree \(>1\); A001 seed has terms up to total degree \(7\)).
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Not claimed]
Polynomial \(q,p\) are not themselves elements of the CCR \(C^*\)-algebra; the
comparison is through regular representations / affiliated fields / Weyl
groups. We do \emph{not} claim every continuous unital \(*\)-endo of the CCR
algebra fails---only Bogoliubov/quasifree. We do \emph{not} claim
\(f\mapsto M_{f\circ F}\) fails as a map \(C_b\to B(L^2)\). Arveson extensions of
the abelian composition map need not implement the momentum part of \(\psi\).
\end{remark}

\section{Construct: Koopman position sector}
\begin{theorem}[Koopman pullback]
Let \(\mathcal{B}_b(\mathbb{R}^3)\) be the abelian von Neumann algebra of
bounded Borel functions of \(Q\). For \(F\) a local diffeomorphism
(\(\det DF\neq 0\) everywhere, in particular the A001 seed), the pullback
\[
\Phi_0(f):=f\circ F,
\qquad f\in\mathcal{B}_b(\mathbb{R}^3),
\]
is a well-defined \textbf{unital normal \(*\)-homomorphism}
\(\mathcal{B}_b(\mathbb{R}^3)\to\mathcal{B}_b(\mathbb{R}^3)\) (hence a normal
unital CP map), for \emph{any} such \(F\)---regardless of properness or
surjectivity.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
\(F\) continuous \(\Rightarrow f\circ F\) Borel and bounded whenever \(f\) is.
Local diffeomorphism \(\Rightarrow\) \(F\) is Lebesgue-nonsingular (preimages
of null sets are null, by the inverse function theorem on a countable cover
of charts), so \(\Phi_0\) descends to a.e.-equivalence classes.
\((f\circ F)^*=\overline{f\circ F}\) and \((fg)\circ F=(f\circ F)(g\circ F)\)
pointwise, hence a.e. A unital \(*\)-homomorphism of von Neumann algebras is
automatically normal and completely positive.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
This resolves, at the position-sector level, the intuition behind the
withdrawn Regular-v2: non-surjectivity of \(F\) does \emph{not} obstruct the
bounded-Borel Koopman pullback. The C0 obstruction (Theorem~5) is specific to
the \emph{vanishing-at-infinity} algebra \(C_0\), not to bounded Borel
functions.
\end{remark}

\section{Open: joint \(q\)--\(p\) extension}
\(\Phi_0\) only captures \(\psi\) on the position generators. Whether some
normal unital CP \(\Phi\) (or Stinespring dilation) extends \(\Phi_0\) to
also reproduce \(\psi(p_j)=\sum_k B_{jk}(Q)p_k\) on a common dense domain,
with no dual-\(F\) translation dynamics required, is
\textbf{not constructed, not ruled out}. See~\texttt{PROGRAM-C-C1d-Koopman-position.md}
for candidate routes and why a unitary composition-operator route is blocked
by T4/G4 while a non-unitary route remains open.

\section{Non-claims}
No channel on \(B(H)\) implementing the full \(\psi\) (position sector only).
No gates/advantage. No dual \(F\)-translations. No ``all quantization fails.''
No full joint \(q\)--\(p\) Stinespring construct.
