\begin{abstract}
We isolate a structural lemma for dual vector fields of local diffeomorphisms
\(F:\mathbb{R}^n\to\mathbb{R}^n\): if global dual fields \(X_j\) with \(DF\cdot X_j=e_j\)
are all complete, then \(F\) is surjective. Hence any non-surjective dual system
has at least one incomplete dual field. We propose a classification schema
(B-incomplete / B-ESS / B-Def / B-CCR / B-CP), populate it on four atlas rows
(identity baseline; exponential half-plane; A001 Keller seed; A002 family pilot),
and record how A001 is a maximal analytic failure mode for one dual generator.
No gates, channels, or computational advantage are claimed. Companion to the
A001 deficiency note~\cite{A001} and the C001 completion obstructions~\cite{C001}.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Cotangent lifts \(\Phi(q,p)=(F(q),J^{-T}p)\) and dual fields \(X_j\) are standard
when \(\det DF\) is nowhere zero. After the A001 analysis of one dual transport
operator~\cite{A001}, one asks which maps admit essentially self-adjoint dual
momenta or standard \(C^*\) packages. This note supplies a structural lemma, a
schema, and worked examples. It does not repair dual-flow dynamics on \(L^2\).

\section{Structural lemma}
\begin{lemma}[Complete dual fields imply surjectivity]
\label{lem:B1}
Let \(F:\mathbb{R}^n\to\mathbb{R}^n\) be \(C^\infty\) and a local diffeomorphism.
Suppose \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) are \(C^\infty\) dual fields, \(DF\cdot X_j=e_j\).
If every \(X_j\) is complete, then \(F(\mathbb{R}^n)=\mathbb{R}^n\).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Along the global flow \(\phi_t^{(j)}\) of \(X_j\),
\(F(\phi_t^{(j)}(q))=F(q)+te_j\). Iterating shows
\(F(\mathbb{R}^n)+\mathbb{R}^n\subset F(\mathbb{R}^n)\). Local diffeomorphism
makes the image open; translation invariance forces surjectivity.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
If dual fields exist and \(F(\mathbb{R}^n)\neq\mathbb{R}^n\), then some \(X_j\) is incomplete.
\end{corollary}

\section{Classification schema}
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\hline
Label & Meaning \\
\hline
B-incomplete & some/all \(X_j\) incomplete \\
B-ESS & some/all \(H_j=-iX_j\) essentially self-adjoint \\
B-Def & deficiency index pattern \\
B-CCR & dual-translation / Bogoliubov-type package \\
B-CP & unital CP / \(C^*\) package for Weyl lift \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Values: \texttt{open} / \texttt{fail} / \texttt{pass} with evidence pointers.
Non-surjective dual systems are \texttt{fail} at B-incomplete by Lemma~\ref{lem:B1}.

\section{Examples}

\begin{example}[A000 --- identity baseline]
\(F=\mathrm{id}\) on \(\mathbb{R}^3\). Dual fields \(\partial_{q_j}\) complete.
B-incomplete \texttt{pass}. Schema sanity check.
\end{example}

\begin{example}[A003 --- exponential half-plane, non-A001]
On \(\mathbb{R}^2\), \(F(x,y)=(x,e^y)\), padded by an idle coordinate in the atlas.
Then \(\det DF=e^y\neq 0\), image \(\mathbb{R}\times(0,\infty)\), dual fields
\(X_0=\partial_x\), \(X_1=e^{-y}\partial_y\). The field \(X_1\) is incomplete
backward (\(e^y=e^{y_0}+t\) hits a wall in finite time). Thus B-incomplete
\texttt{fail}, matching Lemma~\ref{lem:B1}. This is smooth and \emph{not} a
Jacobian-counterexample seed.
\end{example}

\begin{example}[A001 --- Keller seed]
Alp\"oge--Fable seed restated in~\cite{A001}: all three dual fields incomplete;
\(H=-iX_1\) has deficiency indices \((\infty,\infty)\). B-incomplete and B-ESS
\texttt{fail} for that generator; B-CP fails for C0-\(*\) and Bogoliubov packs
in the companion C001 note~\cite{C001}.
\end{example}

\begin{example}[A002 --- family d4 pilot]
Algebraic det/collision pilot certified; real non-surjectivity and dual-field
completeness not yet curve-certified. B-columns remain \texttt{open}
(conditional on geometry).
\end{example}

\section{Discussion}
Lemma~\ref{lem:B1} is the coarse filter. A001 shows a Keller seed can saturate
analytic failure beyond mere incompleteness of one field. A003 shows the
incompleteness half of the lemma on a elementary smooth example. Completion-level
CP questions for Weyl lifts are separated into~\cite{C001}.

\section{Non-claims}
No gates, channels, advantage. No claim every Keller map fails ESS. No dual-flow
repair. A003 is not a Jacobian counterexample.
