## 0. Scope

J5 closed **Joint-Stone-CP-1**: unital normal CP extending \(\Phi_0\) **plus** Stone agreement
\[
\Phi\bigl(e^{is P_1^{\mathrm{Sch}}}\bigr)=e^{is\widetilde H_1}
\]
for some self-adjoint extension \(\widetilde H_1\supset H_1\). The residual left open was:

> unital normal CP extensions of \(\Phi_0\) that **omit** any Stone / SA-momentum axiom.

That residual is **not** a single blob. This note names two packs and settles them:

| Pack | Axioms (sketch) | Verdict |
|------|-----------------|---------|
| **Unitary-Image-CP-1** | unital normal CP on \(M\), \(\Phi|_{\mathcal{B}_b(Q)}=\Phi_0\), \(\Phi(U(s))\) **unitary** for all \(s\) — **no** requirement that the generator extend \(H_1\) | **OBSTRUCT (J6)** |
| **Diag-CP-Φ₀** | unital normal CP on \(M\) (or \(B(H)\)) extending \(\Phi_0\), built as \(\Phi_0\circ E\) for the normal conditional expectation onto positions — **no** unitary Stone image | **CONSTRUCT (J6-C)** |

**Why Unitary-Image-CP-1 is strictly weaker than Joint-Stone-CP-1.**  
J5 requires \(\Phi(U(s))=e^{is\widetilde H_1}\) with \(\widetilde H_1\supset H_1\). J6 drops the dual-field SA link entirely: only that \(\Phi\) send Schrödinger Stone unitaries to *some* unitaries. The geometric kill still applies.

**Why Diag-CP-Φ₀ is not the trivial \(\Phi_0\)-only remark.**  
\(\Phi_0\) alone lives on the position masa. Diag-CP-Φ₀ is a unital normal CP map on the **larger** vNa \(M\) generated by positions **and** \(\{U(s)\}\), extending \(\Phi_0\), with explicit (zero) values on nonzero Fourier modes. It encodes Schrödinger–Heisenberg conjugation of position observables automatically (any extension of \(\Phi_0\) does), without implementing dual momentum.

**Non-claims.** No Joint-Stone success. No full \(\psi\) on \(B(H)\). No gates/channels/advantage. No dual-\(F\) translation dynamics (T4). No claim that every non-unitary-image CP extension recovers \(\psi(p)\). J5 is not weakened: Joint-Stone-CP-1 remains NO-GO; J6 is a different, weaker pack.

## 1. Standing data

Let \(H=L^2(\mathbb{R}^3)\), \(Q=(Q_0,Q_1,Q_2)\) multiplication by coordinates,
\[
U(s):=e^{is P_1^{\mathrm{Sch}}},\qquad P_1^{\mathrm{Sch}}=-i\partial_{q_1},
\]
and \(F=(F_0,F_1,F_2)\) the A001 seed. Write \(\Phi_0(f)=f\circ F\) on \(\mathcal{B}_b(Q)\) (C1d Koopman construct).

Let \(M\) be the von Neumann algebra
\[
M
:=
\bigl\{
f(Q):f\in\mathcal{B}_b(\mathbb{R}^3)
\bigr\}
\vee
\bigl\{
U(s):s\in\mathbb{R}
\bigr\}''
\subset B(H).
\]
Equivalently, \(M\) is the continuous crossed product of the position masa by the \(q_1\)-translation action (Schrödinger representation).

**Joint-Form-Core** (already constructed, J4-F) supplies sesquilinear forms of minimal \(H_j=-iX_j\) on \(C_c^\infty\) together with \(\Phi_0\); it is **reused**, not rebuilt.

## 2. Axiom pack **Unitary-Image-CP-1**

A **Unitary-Image-CP-1 package** is a unital normal completely positive map
\[
\Phi:M\to B(H)
\]
such that:

1. \(\Phi\big|_{\mathcal{B}_b(Q)}=\Phi_0\);
2. \(\Phi\bigl(U(s)\bigr)\) is **unitary** for every \(s\in\mathbb{R}\).

**No** self-adjoint extension of \(H_1\) is named. **No** identity \(\Phi(U(s))=e^{is\widetilde H_1}\) is required — only unitarity of the image.

**Reading.** Joint-Stone-CP-1 \(\Rightarrow\) Unitary-Image-CP-1 (forget that the generator extends \(H_1\)). The converse is false in general: unitarity alone yields some Stone generator \(A\) on \(H\) with \(\Phi(U(s))=e^{is A}\) (if one also assumes strong continuity in \(s\); for J6, pointwise unitarity + multiplicative domain already suffice for the conjugation identity used below).

## 3. Lemma — multiplicative domain (as in J5)

**Standing fact.** For unital CP \(\Phi\), the multiplicative domain
\[
\mathcal{D}_\Phi
=
\bigl\{
a:
\Phi(a^*a)=\Phi(a)^*\Phi(a)
\text{ and }
\Phi(aa^*)=\Phi(a)\Phi(a)^*
\bigr\}
\]
is a \(C^*\)-subalgebra, \(\Phi|_{\mathcal{D}_\Phi}\) is a \(*\)-homomorphism, and
\[
\Phi(xay)=\Phi(x)\,\Phi(a)\,\Phi(y)
\qquad(x,y\in\mathcal{D}_\Phi,\; a\in M).
\]
(Paulsen, *CB maps*, Thm.~3.18; Choi.)

**Lemma J6.1.**  
If \(\Phi\) is a Unitary-Image-CP-1 package, then \(U(s)\in\mathcal{D}_\Phi\) for every \(s\), and
\[
\Phi\bigl(U(s)\,a\,U(s)^*\bigr)
=
\Phi\bigl(U(s)\bigr)\,\Phi(a)\,\Phi\bigl(U(s)\bigr)^*
\qquad\forall a\in M.
\]

**Proof.** Identical to Lemma J5.1: Kadison–Schwarz gives
\(\Phi(U(s))^*\Phi(U(s))\le\Phi(U(s)^*U(s))=I\); unitarity of \(\Phi(U(s))\) forces equality, so \(U(s)\in\mathcal{D}_\Phi\). \(\square\)

**Remark.** \(\mathcal{B}_b(Q)\subset\mathcal{D}_\Phi\) as well, because \(\Phi_0\) is a \(*\)-homomorphism (same as J5 remark).

## 4. Lemma — dual \(F_1\) Heisenberg covariance without SA link

**Lemma J6.2.**  
If \(\Phi\) is a Unitary-Image-CP-1 package and \(V(s):=\Phi(U(s))\) (unitary), then for every \(f\in C_b(\mathbb{R}^3)\) and every \(s\in\mathbb{R}\),
\[
V(s)^*\,(f\circ F)(Q)\,V(s)
=
f\bigl(F_0(Q),\,F_1(Q)+s,\,F_2(Q)\bigr).
\]

**Proof.**  
Schrödinger conjugation: \(U(s)\,f(Q)\,U(s)^*=f_s(Q)\) with \(f_s(q)=f(q_0,q_1+s,q_2)\).  
Apply \(\Phi\). Left side, by Lemma J6.1:
\[
\Phi\bigl(U(s)\,f(Q)\,U(s)^*\bigr)
=
V(s)\,(f\circ F)(Q)\,V(s)^*.
\]
Right side, by \(\Phi|_{\mathcal{B}_b(Q)}=\Phi_0\):
\[
\Phi(f_s(Q))=(f_s\circ F)(Q)=f\bigl(F_0,F_1+s,F_2\bigr)(Q).
\]
Equate and rearrange. \(\square\)

**Reading.** Same covariance formula as J4.1 / J5.2, derived **without** assuming \(\widetilde H_1\supset H_1\).

## 5. Theorem J6 — no Unitary-Image-CP-1 package

**Lemma J6.3 (SvN product structure).**  
Under Unitary-Image-CP-1, writing \(V(s)=\Phi(U(s))\) and assuming \(s\mapsto V(s)\) is strongly continuous (automatic if \(\Phi\) is normal and \(s\mapsto U(s)\) is strongly continuous with \(\Phi\) ultraweakly continuous along the group — alternatively: the three spectral identities of Lemma J6.2 for the coordinate functions already force \((F_1,A)\) to be a regular Schrödinger couple whenever \(V(s)=e^{isA}\)), one obtains a unitary
\[
W:H\to L^2(\mathbb{R})\otimes K
\]
implementing
\[
W F_1 W^*=M_x\otimes I,\quad
W A W^*=P\otimes I,\quad
W F_0 W^*=I\otimes A_0,\quad
W F_2 W^*=I\otimes A_2
\]
as in Lemma J4.2 / J5.3.

**Proof.** Identical to J4.2, using Lemma J6.2 in place of J4.1. \(\square\)

**On strong continuity.**  
If one prefers to avoid a continuity hypothesis on \(V\): the conjugation identity of Lemma J6.2 for all bounded continuous \(f\) already implies that the joint spectral measure of \((F_0,F_1,F_2)\) is invariant under dual-line shifts in the \(F_1\) coordinate after transporting by \(V(s)\). The same \(s\)-independence of Lebesgue density used below follows from that measure-theoretic invariance (cf. Corollary J4.3), without naming a generator \(A\). We record both readings; the geometric contradiction is the same.

**Corollary J6.4.**  
Under Unitary-Image-CP-1, the Lebesgue density of the absolutely continuous part of \(\mu:=F_{\#}\lambda\) is independent of the dual-line coordinate \(s\) a.e.

**Standing geometric lemmas (unchanged from J4).**

- **Lemma J4.4.** Near \((a,c)=(0,2)\), sheet count \(m(a,s,c)=\#F^{-1}(a,s,c)\) equals \(3\) and \(1\) on two positive-measure open slabs \(W\times I_3\) and \(W\times I_1\).
- **Lemma J4.5.** Since \(\lvert\det DF\rvert\equiv 2\), the Lebesgue density of \(\mu\) on \(\operatorname{im} F\) equals \(m/2\).

**Theorem J6.**  
There is **no** Unitary-Image-CP-1 package for the A001 dual-lift data.

**Proof.**  
Assume \(\Phi\) is such a package. By Corollary J6.4 the density \(\rho\) of \(\mu=F_{\#}\lambda\) is \(s\)-independent a.e. By Lemma J4.5, \(\rho=m/2\) on the image, so \(m\) is \(s\)-independent a.e. This contradicts Lemma J4.4. \(\square\)

**Corollary J6.5 (J5 sharpens, not the only kill).**  
Every Joint-Stone-CP-1 package is a Unitary-Image-CP-1 package; thus Theorem J6 **reproves** the J5 obstruction while dropping the axiom \(\widetilde H_1\supset H_1\). The dual-field SA link is inessential to the geometric kill: **unitary image of Schrödinger Stone unitaries + \(\Phi_0\)** already dies.

**Explicit non-claim of J6.**  
J6 does **not** rule out:

1. unital normal CP extensions of \(\Phi_0\) to \(M\) (or \(B(H)\)) for which \(\Phi(U(s))\) fails to be unitary for some \(s\) (see §6 construct);
2. Joint-Form-Core (forms only; already CONSTRUCT);
3. abstract \(C^*\) envelopes of the full polynomial endomorphism \(\psi\) that never contain, or never unitarily implement, \(\{U(s)\}\);
4. non-normal or non-unital positive maps.

## 6. Axiom pack **Diag-CP-Φ₀** — CONSTRUCT

### 6.1 Conditional expectation onto positions

**Lemma J6.6 (normal conditional expectation).**  
There exists a faithful normal conditional expectation
\[
E:M\to\mathcal{B}_b(Q)
\]
(i.e. a unital normal CP projection onto the position masa with the bimodule property \(E(axb)=a\,E(x)\,b\) for \(a,b\in\mathcal{B}_b(Q)\)).

**Proof sketch.**  
The position masa \(\mathcal{A}:=\mathcal{B}_b(Q)\cong L^\infty(\mathbb{R}^3)\) is injective (all abelian von Neumann algebras are injective). Hence a normal conditional expectation \(B(H)\to\mathcal{A}\) exists; restrict to \(M\).  

More explicitly in the crossed-product picture \(M\simeq\mathcal{A}\rtimes_\tau\mathbb{R}\) (translation action in \(q_1\)): amenability of \(\mathbb{R}\) supplies a normal conditional expectation onto the coefficient algebra \(\mathcal{A}\), characterized on a ultraweakly dense \(*\)-subalgebra of finite Fourier sums by
\[
E\Bigl(\sum_{k=1}^N f_k(Q)\,U(s_k)\Bigr)
=
\sum_{s_k=0} f_k(Q)
\]
(well-defined by linear independence of the unitary translates over \(L^\infty\): if \(\sum f_k(Q)U(s_k)=0\) in \(B(H)\), then each \(f_k=0\) a.e.; standard for the Schrödinger representation of the \(ax+b\)/Heisenberg crossed product in one momentum direction). In particular
\[
E\bigl(U(s)\bigr)=0\qquad(s\neq 0),\qquad E\big|_{\mathcal{A}}=\mathrm{id}_{\mathcal{A}}.
\]
\(\square\)

### 6.2 The diagonal CP extension

**Definition (Diag-CP-Φ₀ package).**  
Set
\[
\Phi_{\mathrm{diag}}
:=
\Phi_0\circ E
:M\to B(H).
\]

**Theorem J6-C (Diag-CP-Φ₀ construct).**  
\(\Phi_{\mathrm{diag}}\) is a **unital normal completely positive** map \(M\to B(H)\) such that:

1. \(\Phi_{\mathrm{diag}}\big|_{\mathcal{B}_b(Q)}=\Phi_0\);
2. \(\Phi_{\mathrm{diag}}\bigl(U(s)\bigr)=0\) for all \(s\neq 0\) (hence **not** unitary — no Stone/SA momentum implementation);
3. for every \(f\in\mathcal{B}_b(\mathbb{R}^3)\) and \(s\in\mathbb{R}\),
   \[
   \Phi_{\mathrm{diag}}\bigl(U(s)\,f(Q)\,U(s)^*\bigr)
   =
   (f_s\circ F)(Q)
   =
   f\bigl(F_0,F_1+s,F_2\bigr)(Q),
   \]
   i.e. Schrödinger–Heisenberg conjugates of position observables are pulled back exactly by \(\Phi_0\) (automatic for any extension of \(\Phi_0\), since \(U(s)f(Q)U(s)^*\in\mathcal{B}_b(Q)\)).

**Proof.**  
\(E\) is unital normal CP and a conditional expectation onto \(\mathcal{A}\); \(\Phi_0\) is a unital normal \(*\)-homomorphism \(\mathcal{A}\to B(H)\). Composition preserves unital normal CP. Restriction: \(E|_{\mathcal{A}}=\mathrm{id}\) so \(\Phi_{\mathrm{diag}}|_{\mathcal{A}}=\Phi_0\). Item 2 is \(E(U(s))=0\) for \(s\neq 0\). Item 3: \(U(s)f(Q)U(s)^*=f_s(Q)\in\mathcal{A}\), so
\[
\Phi_{\mathrm{diag}}(f_s(Q))=\Phi_0(f_s)=(f_s\circ F)(Q).
\]
\(\square\)

**Corollary J6.7 (Arveson existence, non-constructive companion).**  
By Arveson’s extension theorem, any unital CP map on the operator system \(\mathcal{B}_b(Q)\subset M\) extends to a unital CP map \(M\to B(H)\). In particular extensions of \(\Phi_0\) to \(M\) exist in abundance. Theorem J6 forces every such extension to send \(U(s)\) to a **non-unitary** for a non-null set of \(s\) (else Unitary-Image-CP-1). Diag-CP-Φ₀ is an **explicit** normal extension realizing that forced non-unitarity on all \(s\neq 0\).

**Reading against Joint-Form-Core.**  
Diag-CP-Φ₀ is a CP map on the joint vNa \(M\); Joint-Form-Core is a form-level package on \(C_c^\infty\) for dual momenta \(H_j\). They are compatible and complementary: forms carry \(\psi(p_j)\) on the core; \(\Phi_{\mathrm{diag}}\) carries \(\Phi_0\) on positions inside \(M\) without Stone. Neither alone is a channel implementing full \(\psi\) on \(B(H)\).

6. J6 does **not** weaken J5: Joint-Stone-CP-1 remains NO-GO on its own axioms; J6 removes the SA-extension axiom and still kills unitary image.
