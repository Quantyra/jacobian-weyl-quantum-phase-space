## 0. Scope

J4 closed the **multiplicative** single-generator pack Joint-Stone-Hom-1. The residual left open was:

> unital normal CP (not necessarily \(*\)-hom) \(\Phi\) extending \(\Phi_0\) on \(\mathcal{B}_b(Q)\), plus some SA extension \(\widetilde H_1\supset H_1\), with \(\Phi(e^{is P_1^{\mathrm{Sch}}})=e^{is\widetilde H_1}\).

No dual-\(F\) translation dynamics as a physical channel (T4). No promotion of G4 \(H_0/H_2\) pairs to A001. No gates/advantage.

## 1. Axiom pack **Joint-Stone-CP-1**

Let \(H=L^2(\mathbb{R}^3)\), \(Q=(Q_0,Q_1,Q_2)\) multiplication by coordinates, and
\[
P_1^{\mathrm{Sch}}=-i\partial_{q_1}
\]
the standard Schrödinger momentum (unique SA closure on \(C_c^\infty\)). Write \(F=(F_0,F_1,F_2)\) for the A001 seed, \(H_1=-iX_1\) the minimal dual momentum on \(C_c^\infty(\mathbb{R}^3)\), and \(\Phi_0(f)=f\circ F\) the Koopman position construct on \(\mathcal{B}_b(Q)\).

Let \(M\) be the von Neumann algebra generated by
\[
\bigl\{f(Q):f\in\mathcal{B}_b(\mathbb{R}^3)\bigr\}
\cup
\bigl\{e^{is P_1^{\mathrm{Sch}}}:s\in\mathbb{R}\bigr\}.
\]

A **Joint-Stone-CP-1 package** is a pair \((\Phi,\widetilde H_1)\) such that:

1. \(\widetilde H_1\) is a self-adjoint extension of \(H_1\);
2. \(\Phi:M\to B(H)\) is a **unital normal completely positive** map (not required to be multiplicative);
3. \(\Phi\big|_{\mathcal{B}_b(Q)}=\Phi_0\), i.e. \(\Phi(f(Q))=f(F(Q))\);
4. **Stone agreement for the single generator \(j=1\):**
   \[
   \Phi\bigl(e^{is P_1^{\mathrm{Sch}}}\bigr)=e^{is\widetilde H_1}\qquad\forall s\in\mathbb{R}.
   \]

**Reading.** Same as residual (A) / Joint-Stone-CP-1 on the J4 scoreboard: choice-dependent single-\(j=1\) Joint-Stone with mere CP in place of Hom.

**Relation to Hom.** Joint-Stone-Hom-1 is the same pack with item 2 strengthened to unital normal \(*\)-homomorphism. J4 already kills Hom. J5 kills the weaker CP pack.

## 2. Lemma — multiplicative domain for Stone unitaries

**Standing fact (multiplicative domain).**  
Let \(\Phi:A\to B(H)\) be a unital completely positive map on a unital \(C^*\)-algebra (or von Neumann algebra). The **multiplicative domain**
\[
\mathcal{D}_\Phi
:=
\bigl\{
a\in A:
\Phi(a^*a)=\Phi(a)^*\Phi(a)
\text{ and }
\Phi(aa^*)=\Phi(a)\Phi(a)^*
\bigr\}
\]
is a \(C^*\)-subalgebra of \(A\), \(\Phi|_{\mathcal{D}_\Phi}\) is a \(*\)-homomorphism, and the **bimodule identity**
\[
\Phi(xay)=\Phi(x)\,\Phi(a)\,\Phi(y)
\qquad\text{for all }x,y\in\mathcal{D}_\Phi,\; a\in A
\]
holds. (Kadison–Schwarz equality set; standard references: Choi; Paulsen, *Completely Bounded Maps and Operator Algebras*, Thm.~3.18.)

**Lemma J5.1 (Stone unitaries lie in \(\mathcal{D}_\Phi\)).**  
If \((\Phi,\widetilde H_1)\) is a Joint-Stone-CP-1 package and \(U(s):=e^{is P_1^{\mathrm{Sch}}}\), \(\widetilde U(s):=e^{is\widetilde H_1}\), then for every \(s\in\mathbb{R}\),
\[
U(s)\in\mathcal{D}_\Phi.
\]
In particular \(\Phi(U(s))=\widetilde U(s)\) is unitary, and
\[
\Phi\bigl(U(s)\,a\,U(s)^*\bigr)
=
\widetilde U(s)\,\Phi(a)\,\widetilde U(s)^*
\qquad\text{for all }a\in M.
\]

**Proof.**  
Unital CP maps are positive and \(*\)-preserving. Kadison–Schwarz gives
\[
\Phi(U(s))^*\Phi(U(s))
\le
\Phi\bigl(U(s)^*U(s)\bigr)
=
\Phi(I)=I.
\]
But \(\Phi(U(s))=\widetilde U(s)\) is unitary, so \(\Phi(U(s))^*\Phi(U(s))=I\). Equality holds in Kadison–Schwarz for \(U(s)\). The same argument with \(U(s)^*=U(-s)\) (or \(aa^*\)) puts \(U(s)\) in \(\mathcal{D}_\Phi\). The bimodule identity with \(x=U(s)\), \(y=U(s)^*\) yields the conjugation formula. \(\square\)

**Remark (position algebra also sits in \(\mathcal{D}_\Phi\)).**  
For \(a=f(Q)\in\mathcal{B}_b(Q)\),
\[
\Phi(a^*a)=\Phi_0(a^*a)=\Phi_0(a)^*\Phi_0(a)=\Phi(a)^*\Phi(a),
\]
since \(\Phi_0\) is a \(*\)-homomorphism. Thus \(\mathcal{B}_b(Q)\subset\mathcal{D}_\Phi\) as well. Combined with Lemma J5.1 this forces \(\Phi\) to be multiplicative on the \(C^*\)-algebra generated by positions and \(\{U(s)\}\); J5 does **not** need that global reduction — covariance alone suffices.

## 3. Lemma — dual \(F_1\) Heisenberg covariance (CP form)

**Lemma J5.2.**  
If \((\Phi,\widetilde H_1)\) is a Joint-Stone-CP-1 package and \(\widetilde U(s):=e^{is\widetilde H_1}\), then for every \(f\in C_b(\mathbb{R}^3)\) and every \(s\in\mathbb{R}\),
\[
\widetilde U(s)^*\,(f\circ F)(Q)\,\widetilde U(s)
=
f\bigl(F_0(Q),\,F_1(Q)+s,\,F_2(Q)\bigr).
\]
In particular,
\begin{align*}
\widetilde U(s)^* F_0(Q)\,\widetilde U(s)&=F_0(Q),\\
\widetilde U(s)^* F_1(Q)\,\widetilde U(s)&=F_1(Q)+s,\\
\widetilde U(s)^* F_2(Q)\,\widetilde U(s)&=F_2(Q).
\end{align*}

**Proof.**  
Schrödinger conjugation gives
\[
U(s)\,f(Q)\,U(s)^*
=
f_s(Q),
\qquad
f_s(q):=f(q_0,q_1+s,q_2).
\]
Apply \(\Phi\). Left side, by Lemma J5.1:
\[
\Phi\bigl(U(s)\,f(Q)\,U(s)^*\bigr)
=
\widetilde U(s)\,(f\circ F)(Q)\,\widetilde U(s)^*.
\]
Right side, by \(\Phi|_{\mathcal{B}_b(Q)}=\Phi_0\):
\[
\Phi(f_s(Q))=(f_s\circ F)(Q)=f\bigl(F_0(Q),F_1(Q)+s,F_2(Q)\bigr).
\]
Equating and rearranging (left-multiply by \(\widetilde U(s)^*\), right-multiply by \(\widetilde U(s)\)) yields the claim. Coordinate projections give the three identities. \(\square\)

**Reading.** This is exactly Lemma J4.1, with multiplicativity of \(\Phi\) replaced by the multiplicative-domain bimodule identity for the Stone unitaries alone.

## 4. Reduction to the J4 geometric chain

**Lemma J5.3 (SvN product structure).**  
Under Joint-Stone-CP-1, the pair \((F_1(Q),\widetilde H_1)\) is a regular Schrödinger couple on \(H\), and \(F_0(Q),F_2(Q)\) lie in the commutant of \(\{F_1(Q),e^{is\widetilde H_1}:s\in\mathbb{R}\}''\). Consequently there exist a separable Hilbert space \(K\) and a unitary
\[
W:H\to L^2(\mathbb{R};K)\cong L^2(\mathbb{R})\otimes K
\]
such that
\begin{align*}
W F_1(Q) W^* &= M_x\otimes I_K,\\
W \widetilde H_1 W^* &= P\otimes I_K,\\
W F_0(Q) W^* &= I\otimes A_0,\\
W F_2(Q) W^* &= I\otimes A_2,
\end{align*}
for self-adjoint operators \(A_0,A_2\) on \(K\).

**Proof.** Identical to Lemma J4.2, using Lemma J5.2 in place of J4.1. \(\square\)

**Corollary J5.4 (s-independent target density).**  
Write \(\mu:=F_{\#}\lambda\). Under Joint-Stone-CP-1, the Lebesgue density of the absolutely continuous part of \(\mu\) is independent of the dual line coordinate \(s\) a.e. (same proof as Corollary J4.3).

**Standing geometric lemmas (from J4, unchanged).**

- **Lemma J4.4 (sheet count varies in \(s\)).** Near \((a,c)=(0,2)\), \(m(a,s,c)=\#F^{-1}(a,s,c)\) takes the constant values \(3\) and \(1\) on two positive-measure open slabs \(W\times I_3\) and \(W\times I_1\).
- **Lemma J4.5.** Since \(\lvert\det DF\rvert\equiv 2\), the Lebesgue density of \(\mu\) on \(\operatorname{im} F\) equals \(m/2\).

## 5. Theorem J5 — no Joint-Stone-CP-1 package

**Theorem J5.**  
There is **no** Joint-Stone-CP-1 package for the A001 dual-lift data.

**Proof.**  
Assume \((\Phi,\widetilde H_1)\) is such a package. By Corollary J5.4 the Lebesgue density \(\rho\) of \(\mu=F_{\#}\lambda\) is independent of \(s\) a.e. By Lemma J4.5, \(\rho=m/2\) on the image. Hence \(m(a,s,c)\) is independent of \(s\) for a.e. \((a,s,c)\). This contradicts Lemma J4.4. \(\square\)

**Corollary J5.5 (CP collapses to Hom on this pack).**  
Every Joint-Stone-CP-1 package would force dual-\(F_1\) Heisenberg covariance and the SvN product structure of Joint-Stone-Hom-1; equivalently, the obstruction is independent of whether multiplicativity of \(\Phi\) is assumed up front or derived from unital CP + unitary Stone agreement via \(\mathcal{D}_\Phi\).

**Explicit non-claim of J5.**  
J5 uses unitality, complete positivity (Kadison–Schwarz / multiplicative domain), normality only insofar as \(\Phi\) is a well-defined normal map on the vNa \(M\), Stone agreement (unitary-to-unitary), and \(\Phi|_{\mathcal{B}_b(Q)}=\Phi_0\). It does **not** rule out:

1. unital normal CP extensions of \(\Phi_0\) that **do not** implement any unitary group \(e^{is\widetilde H_1}\) (no Stone axiom);
2. form-level Joint-Form-Core (already constructed, J4-F);
3. non-unital or non-normal positive maps;
4. abstract \(C^*\) envelopes without Stone / SA-extension axioms;
5. CP packages on algebras that do not contain both \(\mathcal{B}_b(Q)\) and \(\{e^{is P_1^{\mathrm{Sch}}}\}\).
