## 1. Axiom pack **Joint-Stone-Hom-1**

Let \(H=L^2(\mathbb{R}^3)\), \(Q=(Q_0,Q_1,Q_2)\) multiplication by coordinates, and
\[
P_1^{\mathrm{Sch}}=-i\partial_{q_1}
\]
the standard Schrödinger momentum (unique SA closure on \(C_c^\infty\)). Write \(F=(F_0,F_1,F_2)\) for the A001 seed, \(H_1=-iX_1\) the minimal dual momentum on \(C_c^\infty(\mathbb{R}^3)\), and \(\Phi_0(f)=f\circ F\) the Koopman position construct on \(\mathcal{B}_b(Q)\).

Let \(M\) be the von Neumann algebra generated by
\[
\bigl\{f(Q):f\in\mathcal{B}_b(\mathbb{R}^3)\bigr\}
\cup
\bigl\{e^{is P_1^{\mathrm{Sch}}}:s\in\mathbb{R}\bigr\}.
\]

A **Joint-Stone-Hom-1 package** is a pair \((\Phi,\widetilde H_1)\) such that:

1. \(\widetilde H_1\) is a self-adjoint extension of \(H_1\);
2. \(\Phi:M\to B(H)\) is a **unital normal \(*\)-homomorphism** (multiplicative — stronger than mere CP);
3. \(\Phi\big|_{\mathcal{B}_b(Q)}=\Phi_0\), i.e. \(\Phi(f(Q))=f(F(Q))\);
4. **Stone agreement for the single generator \(j=1\):**
   \[
   \Phi\bigl(e^{is P_1^{\mathrm{Sch}}}\bigr)=e^{is\widetilde H_1}\qquad\forall s\in\mathbb{R}.
   \]

**Reading.** This is the choice-dependent single-\(j=1\) Joint-Stone pack with the **named extra axiom** that \(\Phi\) is a \(*\)-homomorphism (Q–P cross-commutation is preserved, not merely CP-interpolated).

## 2. Lemma — dual \(F_1\) Heisenberg covariance

**Lemma J4.1.**  
If \((\Phi,\widetilde H_1)\) is a Joint-Stone-Hom-1 package and \(U(s):=e^{is\widetilde H_1}\), then for every \(f\in C_b(\mathbb{R}^3)\) and every \(s\in\mathbb{R}\),
\[
U(s)^*\,(f\circ F)(Q)\,U(s)
=
f\bigl(F_0(Q),\,F_1(Q)+s,\,F_2(Q)\bigr).
\]
In particular,
\begin{align*}
U(s)^* F_0(Q)\,U(s)&=F_0(Q),\\
U(s)^* F_1(Q)\,U(s)&=F_1(Q)+s,\\
U(s)^* F_2(Q)\,U(s)&=F_2(Q).
\end{align*}

**Proof.**  
Schrödinger conjugation gives
\[
e^{is P_1^{\mathrm{Sch}}\,f(Q)\,e^{-is P_1^{\mathrm{Sch}}}
=
f(Q_0,\,Q_1+s,\,Q_2).
\]
Write \(f_s(q):=f(q_0,q_1+s,q_2)\), so the right-hand side is \(f_s(Q)\). Apply the \(*\)-homomorphism \(\Phi\):
\[
\Phi\bigl(e^{is P_1} f(Q) e^{-is P_1}\bigr)
=
\Phi(e^{is P_1})\,\Phi(f(Q))\,\Phi(e^{-is P_1})
=
U(s)\,(f\circ F)(Q)\,U(s)^*,
\]
while
\[
\Phi(f_s(Q))=(f_s\circ F)(Q)=f\bigl(F_0(Q),F_1(Q)+s,F_2(Q)\bigr).
\]
Equating and rearranging yields the claim. Coordinate projections give the three identities. \(\square\)

## 3. Lemma — Stone–von Neumann product structure

**Lemma J4.2.**  
Under Joint-Stone-Hom-1, the pair \((F_1(Q),\widetilde H_1)\) is a regular Schrödinger couple on \(H\), and \(F_0(Q),F_2(Q)\) lie in the commutant of \(\{F_1(Q),e^{is\widetilde H_1}:s\in\mathbb{R}\}''\). Consequently there exist a separable Hilbert space \(K\) and a unitary
\[
W:H\to L^2(\mathbb{R};K)\cong L^2(\mathbb{R})\otimes K
\]
such that
\begin{align*}
W F_1(Q) W^* &= M_x\otimes I_K,\\
W \widetilde H_1 W^* &= P\otimes I_K,\\
W F_0(Q) W^* &= I\otimes A_0,\\
W F_2(Q) W^* &= I\otimes A_2,
\end{align*}
for self-adjoint operators \(A_0,A_2\) on \(K\). (Here \(P=-i\partial_x\) on \(L^2(\mathbb{R})\) with its usual SA domain.)

**Proof.**  
Lemma J4.1 gives the Weyl form of the CCR between \(F_1\) and \(\widetilde H_1\), so Stone–von Neumann supplies the first two lines (constant multiplicity). The identities \(U(s)^*F_j U(s)=F_j\) for \(j=0,2\) say that \(F_0,F_2\) commute with the full Weyl system generated by \((F_1,\widetilde H_1)\). Schur’s lemma for the irreducible Schrödinger representation on \(L^2(\mathbb{R})\) forces \(W F_j W^*\) to act as \(I\otimes A_j\) on the multiplicity space. \(\square\)

**Corollary J4.3 (s-independent target density).**  
Write \(\mu:=F_{\#}\lambda\) for the pushforward of Lebesgue measure \(\lambda\) on \(\mathbb{R}^3_q\) under the seed map \(F\). Under Joint-Stone-Hom-1, the measure class of \(\mu\) admits a Radon–Nikodym density \(\rho(a,s,c)\) w.r.t. Lebesgue on \(\mathbb{R}^3_{a,s,c}\) (on the absolutely continuous part) that is **independent of \(s\)** for \(\lambda\)-a.e. \((a,s,c)\).

**Proof.**  
In the coordinates of Lemma J4.2, if \(K=L^2(X,\nu)\) then
\[
(F_0,F_1,F_2)
\sim
\bigl(a(x),\,x_{\mathrm{line}},\,c(x)\bigr)
\]
with spectral measure of product type \(\mathrm{d}s\,\nu(\mathrm{d}x)\) pushed forward by \(x\mapsto(a(x),c(x))\). The resulting density on target space does not depend on the line coordinate \(s\). \(\square\)

## 4. Lemma — A001 preimage cardinality depends on \(s\)

**Lemma J4.4 (sheet count varies in \(s\)).**  
Let \(m(a,s,c):=\# F^{-1}(a,s,c)\) be the number of real preimages (finite for the A001 polynomial seed off a Zariski-null branch locus). Then there exist a nonempty open set \(W\subset\mathbb{R}^2_{a,c}\) and nonempty open intervals \(I_3,I_1\subset\mathbb{R}_s\) such that
\[
m(a,s,c)=3\quad\text{for all }(a,c)\in W,\; s\in I_3,
\qquad
m(a,s,c)=1\quad\text{for all }(a,c)\in W,\; s\in I_1.
\]
In particular \(m(\,\cdot\,,s,\,\cdot\,)\) is **not** essentially independent of \(s\).

**Proof / witnesses.**  
At the base point \((a,c)=(0,2)\):

| \(s\) | \(m(0,s,2)\) |
|------:|:------------:|
| \(-1.00\) | \(3\) |
| \(0.00\) | \(1\) |
| \(0.25\) | \(3\) |
| \(0.49\) | \(3\) |
| \(0.50\) | \(1\) |
| \(0.70\) | \(1\) |
| \(1.00\) | \(1\) |
| \(2.00\) | \(1\) |

(Symmetric pattern: \(m=3\) throughout \((-1,0)\cup(0,\tfrac12)\) at sample resolution \(0.05\), and \(m=1\) throughout \((\tfrac12,2]\).)  
Real roots of the eliminant cubic (A001 flow-box equation \(A(s;a,c)q_0^3+B(s;c)q_0+C(c)=0\)) depend continuously on \((a,s,c)\) off the discriminant locus where roots collide or escape. Hence the open \(s\)-intervals on which \(m(0,\,\cdot\,,2)=3\) and \(=1\) persist on a small open neighbourhood \(W\ni(0,2)\) in the \((a,c)\)-plane. Explicit check: at \((a,c)=(0,2.01)\) one still has \(m(0.25)=3\) and \(m(0.7)=1\). \(\square\)

**Lemma J4.5 (change of variables).**  
Since \(F\) is a global local diffeomorphism (\(\det DF\equiv-2\neq 0\)),
\[
\mu=F_{\#}\lambda
=
\frac{m}{2}\,\lambda\Big|_{\operatorname{im} F}
\]
as measures on \(\mathbb{R}^3\) (sum of the \(1/|\det DF|\) contributions over smooth sheets). Thus the Lebesgue density of \(\mu\) on the image is \(m/2\).

## 5. Theorem J4 — no Joint-Stone-Hom-1 package

**Theorem J4.**  
There is **no** Joint-Stone-Hom-1 package for the A001 dual-lift data.

**Proof.**  
Assume \((\Phi,\widetilde H_1)\) is such a package. By Corollary J4.3 the Lebesgue density \(\rho\) of \(\mu=F_{\#}\lambda\) is independent of \(s\) a.e. By Lemma J4.5, \(\rho=m/2\) on the image. Hence \(m(a,s,c)\) is independent of \(s\) for a.e. \((a,s,c)\). This contradicts Lemma J4.4, where \(m\) takes the distinct constant values \(3\) and \(1\) on two positive-measure open slabs \(W\times I_3\) and \(W\times I_1\). \(\square\)

**Explicit non-claim of J4 (historical; closed by J5).**  
J4 uses multiplicativity of \(\Phi\) **as an axiom** (to obtain dual-\(F_1\) Heisenberg covariance and then SvN product structure). The mere-CP pack with the same Stone axiom is **not** left open: Theorem J5 derives the same covariance from the multiplicative domain of a unital CP map and applies the same geometric kill (`PROGRAM-C-residual-mere-CP-Joint-Stone.md`).

## 6. Side residual: Joint-Form split

### 6.1 Pack **Joint-Form-Core** — CONSTRUCT

**Data.** Dense core \(D=C_c^\infty(\mathbb{R}^3)\); Koopman \(\Phi_0\) on positions; for each \(j\in\{0,1,2\}\) the sesquilinear form
\[
\mathfrak{q}_j(u,v):=\langle u, H_j v\rangle_H,
\qquad u,v\in D,
\]
with \(H_j=-iX_j\) the minimal dual momentum.

**Theorem J4-F (Joint-Form-Core construct).**  
For every \(j\), \(\mathfrak{q}_j\) coincides on \(D\) with the Schrödinger quantization of the algebraic momentum assignment \(\psi(p_j)=\sum_k B_{jk}(q)\,p_k\) (Piola / Weyl ordering already built into \(H_j=-iX_j\) on the div-free dual frame). Together with \(\Phi_0\), this is a **Joint-Form-Core** package: form-level joint agreement on a common dense core, with **no** SA-extension or Stone requirement.

**Proof.**  
By construction of the dual fields \(X_j=\sum_k B_{jk}\partial_{q_k}\) from \(B=J^{-T}\) and \(\operatorname{div} X_j=0\) (A001 / G3), the symmetric operator \(H_j=-iX_j\) on \(C_c^\infty\) **is** the Schrödinger representative of \(\psi(p_j)\). The identity of forms is tautological on \(D\). \(\Phi_0\) is already constructed (C1d). \(\square\)

**Reading.** This is the weakest honest Joint-Form pack. It does **not** produce unitary groups, CP joint extensions, or preferred boundary conditions.

### 6.2 Pack **Joint-Form-ESS-1** — OBSTRUCT

**Extra axiom:** the form \(\mathfrak{q}_1\) is required to be represented by an **essentially self-adjoint** operator on \(D\) (unique SA representation).

**Theorem J4-E.**  
Joint-Form-ESS-1 fails for A001: \(H_1\) is not essentially self-adjoint on \(C_c^\infty(\mathbb{R}^3)\) (A001 Theorems E–F: \((n_+,n_-)=(\infty,\infty)\)).
